[Verse 1]

For two hundred years...

the arithmetic balanced.

Heat...
Work...

Equal signs standing
like fence posts
across the landscape.

The engine never complained.

The equations behaved.

But sometimes...

a map arrives
long before
the language
to describe the country.

[Verse 2]

Imagine a marble
rolling through sunlight.

You see its shadow.

You measure its speed.

You know its weight.

Yet buried
inside the stone...

is a color
the shadow
can never confess.

Question is...

Can you discover
the unseen...

by studying
what it leaves behind?

[Pre-Chorus]

Every mystery
casts evidence.

The invisible
is rarely silent.

[Chorus]

There's a geometry
beneath the fire.

A hidden country
inside desire.

Heat isn't lost...

It simply lives
where light can't go.

Every shadow
writes a story...

only mathematics
learns to know.

[Verse 3]

Work
shakes your hand.

Heat...

leaves fingerprints.

One can move a piston.

The other
slips quietly
through every bargain
nature ever made.

Different voices...

Same conversation.

[Bridge – spoken]

Gauge theory...

Funny phrase.

Sounds like something
you'd borrow
from an old mechanic.

Turns out...

it's how physicists
keep track
of things
that refuse
to stand still.

Maybe the universe
has never hidden
its secrets.

Maybe...

we've been reading
the shadow...

instead of
the object.

Alright...

Alright...

Alright...

[Verse 4]

Entropy...

Perhaps it was never
disorder.

Perhaps...

it's perspective.

Stand too close...

Life looks like chaos.

Step back...

Geometry
begins to rhyme.

Even a black hole...

might be speaking
the very same language
as a steam engine.

[Final Chorus]

There's a geometry
beneath the flame.

Every answer
changing names.

The visible world
is only the verse...

The hidden world
completes the chorus.

Two hundred years...

One deeper view.

Not a new universe...

A new way
to see the old one.

[Outro – spoken]

Science isn't
the business
of replacing certainty.

It's the art...

of discovering...

that certainty
was merely
the shadow...

of a better question.
